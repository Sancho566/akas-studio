Project Overview

TechHub approached us to design their new headquarters that would reflect their innovative culture while accommodating rapid growth. The challenge was to create a space that balanced open collaboration areas with focused work zones.

Our solution incorporated flexible workspaces, cutting-edge technology integration, and biophilic design elements to create an environment that fosters creativity and productivity.

The Challenge

  • Accommodate 120 employees with room to grow
  • Create distinct zones for different work modes
  • Incorporate brand identity into the space
  • Implement sustainable design practices

Design Details

Every element was carefully considered to create a cohesive and functional workspace.

Collaboration Zones

The collaboration zones feature modular furniture that can be reconfigured for different team sizes and project needs. Whiteboard walls and integrated technology support brainstorming sessions.

Focus Pods

Soundproof focus pods provide quiet spaces for concentrated work or private calls. Each pod is equipped with ergonomic seating, adjustable lighting, and power outlets.

Breakout Areas

Casual breakout areas with comfortable seating and greenery create spaces for informal meetings or relaxation. These areas feature the brand's color palette in a subtle, sophisticated way.

Tech-Enabled Meeting Rooms

All meeting rooms feature state-of-the-art video conferencing systems, smart whiteboards, and intuitive room booking displays. Acoustic treatments ensure clear communication.

Smart Technology Integration

The workspace features cutting-edge technology seamlessly integrated into the design:

  • Enterprise-Grade WiFi

    Full coverage with multiple access points for uninterrupted connectivity.

  • Room Booking System

    Digital displays outside each room show real-time availability.

  • Smart Lighting

    Automated systems adjust based on occupancy and natural light levels.

Sustainable Design Approach

The project achieved LEED Gold certification through thoughtful material selection and energy-efficient systems.

Energy Efficiency

LED lighting with motion sensors, high-performance glazing, and optimized HVAC systems reduce energy consumption by 35%.

Material Selection

Over 75% of materials were sourced locally or contain recycled content, including reclaimed wood and recycled metal.

Biophilic Design

Living walls, abundant natural light, and indoor plants improve air quality and employee wellbeing.
